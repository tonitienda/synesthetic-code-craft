# 03 — Beat Sheet

## Act I — The First Artificial Neuron

---

## Beat 1 — Start with the mystery

### Purpose

Open with backpropagation, but do not explain it yet.

### Visual idea

Title appears in empty space.

### Narration idea

"Backpropagation. How neural networks learn from mistakes."

### Question created

Where does this story begin?

---

## Beat 2 — Rewind to the perceptron

### Purpose

Introduce the idea that the story begins with a single learning unit.

### Visual idea

The title fades. A single circle appears.

### Narration idea

"But the story does not start with a deep network. It starts with a single artificial neuron."

### Question created

What is an artificial neuron?

---

## Beat 3 — Historical anchor

### Purpose

Ground the perceptron historically.

### Visual idea

A minimal portrait placeholder and date appear: Frank Rosenblatt, 1958.

### Narration idea

"In the late nineteen fifties, Frank Rosenblatt introduced the perceptron."

### Question created

Why was it exciting?

---

## Beat 4 — Inputs arrive

### Purpose

Show the neuron receiving information.

### Visual idea

Three lines grow from the left into the circle.

### Narration idea

"The perceptron receives input values. Each input is just a number."

### Question created

Do all inputs matter equally?

---

## Beat 5 — Weights appear

### Purpose

Introduce weights as importance controls.

### Visual idea

Labels w₁, w₂, w₃ appear on the input connections. Line thickness changes.

### Narration idea

"Each connection has a weight. The weight controls how strongly that input matters."

### Question created

What does the neuron do with weighted inputs?

---

## Beat 6 — The neuron combines evidence

### Purpose

Show the weighted sum.

### Visual idea

Small signal dots travel along the input lines. Inside the neuron, a sigma symbol appears.

### Narration idea

"The neuron multiplies each input by its weight, then combines the results."

### Question created

How does this become a decision?

---

## Beat 7 — Output decision

### Purpose

Show activation and output.

### Visual idea

A threshold line appears inside or near the neuron. An output line grows to the right.

### Narration idea

"If the combined signal is strong enough, the neuron activates."

### Question created

How can this learn?

---

## Beat 8 — Learning as weight change

### Purpose

Introduce learning without gradients yet.

### Visual idea

One weight increases. The corresponding line becomes thicker. The output changes.

### Narration idea

"Changing a weight changes the decision. This is the seed of learning."

### Question created

What kind of problems can this solve?

---

## Beat 9 — Decision boundary

### Purpose

Translate the perceptron into geometry.

### Visual idea

The neuron diagram transforms into a 2D graph with red and blue points separated by a line.

### Narration idea

"With two inputs, a perceptron draws a line between two classes."

### Question created

Can one line solve every problem?

---

## Beat 10 — The limitation

### Purpose

End Act I with a limitation.

### Visual idea

The line tries to rotate and move, but cannot separate a more complex point pattern.

### Narration idea

"This is powerful, but limited. A single perceptron can only draw a linear boundary."

### Act II hook

"So what happens if one line is not enough?"
