## Notes

- Music starts right at the beginning. Splash screen is the first thing
- PI (the mascot) appears
- First 20 seconds: the question is raised. how does the brain understands a slopy 3 as a 3?
- Animations are rich: the different representations of "3" travel to the eye of the mascot while the narration says "the cells in your eyes that activate for this 3 or that 3 are different"
- 0:46 first new "scene" but there is an element that although is new, feels like continuity: one grid with the number 3
- 1:28 still feels like an intro. Defining the goal of the video: "I want you to understand ..."
- The screen is a bit crowded for my taste.
- 2:39 still explaining the scope of the video. Not explaining convolutional, etc. just vanilla multilayer perceptron
  - Too much intro for my taste. It feels like really a podcast or someone taking to a group of people, improvised and adding an animation on top
- 2:48 the video "starts"
- Concepts are very layman: lit. "neuron is a thing that holds a number"
  - While seeing this the number changes 0.8 -> 0.4 -> 0.6 . Illustrated it can get any number and making the image alive without animations or real data.
- Animation from a matrix of numbers (the grayscale of the drawn number) to the first layer of neurons was cool:
  - Expand rows vertically
  - Expand horizontally (top most stays, second moves a little, third moves more, etc)
  - They align and become a full row of "pixels/neurons" (circles)
  - Turn into oooo...ooooo (784)
  - Rotate vertically
  - Layers and connections are added
- Explain last layer: 10 neurons (each representing one number/digit)
  - Use the same grayscale values (0 black, 1 white) to represent the output (probability of each number)

In general the components and animations are good to convey the idea and do not look very elaborated.
That makes me think that I am making components too complex (shade/light, bouncy, etc) while they could be simpler and minimal.
