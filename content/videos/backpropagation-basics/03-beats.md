# Beat Sheet — Act I

## Beat 1 — Backpropagation starts before backpropagation

### Goal

Open with the title, then immediately move backwards in the story.

### Viewer question

Why are we starting with a single neuron?

### Transition

To understand how deep networks learn, first understand the smallest learning unit.

---

## Beat 2 — The perceptron appears

### Goal

Introduce Frank Rosenblatt and the perceptron.

### Viewer question

What made this idea exciting?

### Transition

It was exciting because it could learn from examples.

---

## Beat 3 — A neuron receives inputs

### Goal

Show the neuron as a circle and input connections as lines.

### Viewer question

What are these lines carrying?

### Transition

They carry numbers.

---

## Beat 4 — Inputs have weights

### Goal

Show that not all inputs matter equally.

### Viewer question

Who decides which input matters more?

### Transition

The weights do, and learning changes them.

---

## Beat 5 — The neuron makes a decision

### Goal

Show weighted inputs entering the neuron, being combined, and producing an output.

### Viewer question

How does this become a decision boundary?

### Transition

For two inputs, the decision becomes a line.

---

## Beat 6 — Learning means moving the line

### Goal

Show that changing weights moves or rotates the decision boundary.

### Viewer question

Can a line solve every classification problem?

### Transition

No. Some problems cannot be separated by one line.

---

## Beat 7 — The limitation

### Goal

End with the idea that a single perceptron is linear.

### Viewer question

What if we connect many perceptrons?

### Transition to Act II

The next act introduces XOR and hidden layers.
