# Treatment — Act I

## Video title

Backpropagation: How Neural Networks Learn From Mistakes

## Act title

The First Artificial Neuron

## Audience

Software engineers who are curious about AI internals but do not necessarily have a machine learning background.

## Emotional goal

The viewer should feel that the perceptron is simple, elegant, and historically exciting.

Then the viewer should feel the first crack:

> this is powerful, but maybe too simple.

## Conceptual goal

By the end of Act I, the viewer understands:

- a perceptron receives numbers as inputs
- inputs are multiplied by weights
- the weighted values are combined
- the result produces a decision
- changing weights changes the decision
- this is the seed of learning
- a single perceptron can only draw a straight decision boundary

## Tone

Calm.
Precise.
Curious.
Minimal.
Beautiful.

No hype.
No exaggerated AI claims.
No fast news-channel energy.

## Visual style

Dark background.
Soft glowing nodes.
Thin lines.
Careful spacing.
Motion should reveal the concept step by step.

Avoid crowded diagrams.

## Story

We begin with the word backpropagation, but we immediately step backwards in time.

Before deep networks, before transformers, before modern AI systems, there was a simpler question:

> Could a machine learn from examples?

The perceptron appears as the first hero of the story.

It is not magical. It is a small machine that receives numbers, gives them importance, combines them, and produces a decision.

Then the act reveals the key insight:

> if changing weights changes the decision, then learning can be understood as changing weights.

But the act does not end in triumph.

It ends with a limitation:

> a single perceptron can only separate data with a straight line.

This creates the question for Act II.

## Ending question

If one neuron is not enough, what happens if we connect many?
